Miruthula Jegadesan ~

Miruthula Jegadesan started learning Bharathnatyam in the Kalakshetra Bani (style) in 2005 at the age of 9 from Smt. Preeti Anand at Samarpan School of Dance. She is her guru's first student. Miruthula successfully completed her Bharathnatyam Rangapravesham (Arangetram) on August 10, 2014 at Chandler Center for the Arts, Arizona. She has performed on various stages in Arizona. Miruthula has had the opportunity to perform for the fine Arts Association of Arizona, Maha Ganapathi temple of Arizona, Venkata Krishna Shetra Temple and has been part of many fundraising dance productions for noble causes.She did her first 1 hour solo performance at her brother's Upanayanam in India in the year 2011. Miruthula has choreographed dances for other emerging dancers in her community for events conducted by local organizations such as Arizona Tamil Sangam, Indo American Foundation of Arizona, and Kerala Hindus Association of Arizona. Miruthula currently takes advance training from her guru and also teaches a class at her guru's dance school.

Inspirations

Preeti Anand

Preeti Anand (my guru) started her intial training from Smt. Usha Srinivasan (Disciple of Sri Dandayudhapani Pillai)and continued her training under Sri M S Hariharan and Smt. Pramila Hariharan (Kalakshetra, Chennai), she performed her Arangetram in 1994 and has given numerous performances all over India, both solo and group. Samarpan School of Dance was started by Preeti Anand in Chandler in 2005.

Leela Samson

Leela Samson is a dancer, teacher, writer and choreographer of bharatanatyam. Over years of independent work, her dance has metamorphosed from representing the best of her alma mater into a unique personal expression, which is at once unostentatious, serene, philosophical and joyful. Leela has been the subject of two documentary films, Sanchari and The Flowering Tree. She has also authored several articles as well as two books, Rhythm in Joy (1987) and Rukmini Devi: A Life (2010). She is the recipient of several honours, from the Padmashri and Kalaimamani to the Sangeet Natak Akademi and Sanskriti awards. Leela has served as Director of the Kalakshetra Foundation, as Chairperson of the Sangeet Natak Akademi as well as of the Central Board of Film Certification. -(http://www.leelasamsondance.com/leela/personal_profile/profile.html)

Rukmini Vijaykumar

Rukmini Vijaykumer had an initial exposure to ballet, but her primary training and rigorous practice through her initial years was in Bharatanatyam. She had her Arangetram, (debut, solo performance) in 1998 under Guru Padmini Rao, after which she continued her practice under Guru Narmada, (a recipient of the Sangeet Natak Academy Award) and Guru Sundari Santhanam (a senior disciple Dr Padma Subrahmanyam). She was introduced to the teachings of the Natyashastra, and pursued an intense training in the Karanas under Guru Sundari Santhanam for many years. She furthered her knowledge of Bharatanatyam by studying, Sanskrit, Tala (rhythm) in music, and Yoga through her teenage years. -(http://thiraseela.com/artist/profile.php?perfmrid=171)

Apoorva Jayaraman

Apoorva Jayaraman is a disciple of Smt.Priyadarsini Govind, who is currently the director of Kalakshetra. She first performed solo at the age of 8 in India.
